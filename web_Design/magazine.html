Brazil vs Senegal at Emirates: a night that felt bigger than a friendly

On 15 November, I went to Emirates Stadium to watch Brazil vs Senegal in a FIFA International Friendly. Even though it was “only” a friendly, the atmosphere didn’t feel small at all. From outside the stadium to the moment the teams stepped onto the pitch, it felt like a real football event.

The first thing I noticed was the sound. Chants started in one section and travelled across the stands. When Brazil moved forward with the ball, you could feel the crowd’s attention change instantly. Watching live football is different because you see the full picture: the positioning, the runs off the ball, and how quickly teams react when possession changes.

I support Brazil internationally, mainly because Neymar is my all-time favourite player. Neymar is the kind of player that makes football exciting — creative, confident, and always trying something. Seeing a team you support in a live stadium setting makes the match feel personal, not just entertainment.

Senegal also made the match intense. Their energy and physical style created real pressure, and it kept the game competitive. The crowd reacted to every big tackle and every quick counter-attack. Moments that look normal on TV feel bigger in real life, because thousands of people react at the same time.

As a Manchester United supporter, nights like this remind me why football matters. Club football is my weekly routine, but international matches have a different feeling — more flags, more mixed supporters, and a stronger “event” atmosphere. My favourite United player is Bruno Fernandes, because he takes responsibility and often feels like the player carrying the team every season. That mindset — leadership and effort — is something I respect in football at every level.

Live football is not only about the final score — it’s about the atmosphere, the tension, and the memory you take home.
